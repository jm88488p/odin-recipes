Coquito Recipe

Description

Coquito is a traditional holiday drink that originated in Puerto Rico. In Spanish, coquito means "little coconut." The beverage is coconut-based and spiked with rum. Rich and creamy coquito is similar to eggnog. In fact, sometimes it's referred to as "Puerto Rican eggnog."

Coquito is a creamy, tropical, Puerto Rican holiday drink with tons of coconut flavor. It's made with both evaporated and condensed milk, cream of coconut, spices, and rum.

Coquito only takes about 15 minutes to make.

Ingredients

  • Evaporated milk
  • Condensed milk
  • Coconut milk
  • Coconut Cream
  • Spices
  • Rum
  • Vanilla extract
  • Water

Steps

  1. Gather all neccessary ingredients
  2. Measure cinnamon, cloves, allspice, star anise, and nutmeg with your heart
  3. Place spices in water to boil for 5 minutes then let simmer for 10 minutes
  4. Pour 1 can of coconut cream in large bowl
  5. Pour 1 can of hot tea into the large bowl and whisk until Coconut cream is broken down
  6. While still hot add 1 can of condensed milk and whisk until completely mixed in
  7. Add 1 can Coconut milk & 1 can evaporated milk and whisk into the bowl
  8. Add extra ground cinnamon 1-2 teaspoons and let cool for 5 minutes at room temp
  9. when completely cool add 1 tablespooon vanilla extract
  10. Add 1- 1 1/2 cups of Rum or Spiced Rum if you want it to be spiked
  11. Lastly bottle in a glass bottle with cinnamon sticks for storage
